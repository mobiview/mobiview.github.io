Building an Inclusive Web

Accessibility (A11y) is not a "bonus feature"—it is a legal and ethical requirement for the modern internet. The **A11y Checker** allows you to see your site through the eyes of users with different abilities. Whether it is ensuring high contrast for users with low vision or proper DOM order for screen readers, testing is the only way to guarantee access.

How to Audit for Accessibility

  1. Visual Simulation: Use the "Grayscale" filter to see if your design relies solely on color to convey meaning (e.g., error messages that are only red).
  2. Structure Check: Verify that your heading levels follow a logical hierarchy. This helps assistive technologies navigate your content.
  3. Interactive Elements: Ensure that all buttons and links have distinct focus states and clear ARIA labels if they do not contain text.
